Marine Paintings
Taking inspiration from both historic and contemporary maritime scenes, I paint subjects ranging from the early age of sail and Nelson's era to modern day yachts and cruise ships. My work has been exhibited alongside leading marine artists through Marine Artists Gallery, as well as with the Royal Society of Marine Artists.

In 2023 I was part of the Ocean Globe Race village in Southampton, creating paintings now in the collection of sailors including Tracy Edwards and Marco Trombetti. I'm continuing to depict a broad range of subjects, with a current focus on vessels of exploration and the colourful landscapes they visited.

Commissions
Commissioning an oil painting is a straightforward process, guided at your pace. I'm happy to paint vessels from any era, and am open to discussing ideas for different subjects including aviation and landscape.
‍
Historic vessels are reconstructed from research and the ship's plans - modern vessels can also be drawn from plans / general arrangement, with photographs used as reference for details. Discussions are always welcome, and there is no obligation at the enquiry stage.
1. Discuss Subjects
We start with a conversation around the subjects you're interested in, and cover practical details like the size of the painting.
2  Sketches
Small sketches are prepared to explore viewpoints, which you can then review and suggest changes to. These mature into the full size drawing that is the basis of the painting.
3. Painting
The painting is completed over 3-5 layers, with progress updates at every point.
4. Shipping
Once finished, the painting is varnished for protection. Framing can be arranged too. The completed piece is delivered securely by courier, or can be collected from Ocean Village, Southampton.
